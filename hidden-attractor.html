Hidden Attractor

Hidden attractors are attractors whose basins of attraction are not connected with small neighborhoods of equilibrium points. This makes them fundamentally different from self-excited attractors, which can usually be found by starting trajectories near unstable equilibria. Because of this property, hidden attractors are often difficult to locate, and their generation mechanisms remain unclear. In our ongoing work, we aim to further explore how hidden attractors arise in nonlinear dynamical systems and to better understand the mechanisms behind their formation.
